Reduced NI's product documentation library from 1.1 million pages to 250,000 — a 77% reduction — through a research-driven evergreen content model. The result: improved search findability, stronger SEO, and a consistent documentation experience across a portfolio of nearly 5,000 products.

---

## The Challenge

Over two decades, NI's documentation had grown without a governing architecture. Each product team created content independently, resulting in a fragmented library of millions of pages with no predictable structure, no consistent naming, and no shared navigation patterns.

The consequences were significant. Documentation pages competed directly with product pages in search results — both appearing under the same title, such as "What is LabVIEW?" — splitting authority and confusing customers. Users couldn't reliably find what they needed because every product had organized content differently. The organization had no durable answer to the question: *what does a complete, well-structured documentation set look like?*

---

## The Approach

Rather than designing a solution from assumption, I led user research to understand how customers actually found and consumed documentation. The research surfaced three key insights:

1. Customers search at the **product model level** — not by topic or feature name.
2. Customers expect a single authoritative **"Manual"** as the primary reference.
3. **Specifications** are treated as a distinct, standalone document — not sections buried inside a larger guide.

These findings drove the design of the evergreen content model: one User Manual per product, plus a standalone Specifications document. The model replaced the prior fragmentation of Getting Started Guides, Feature Usage docs, Process docs, and other ad hoc document types.

With the model defined, I drove standardization across all hardware and software product lines — coordinating implementation across a large team of technical writers, establishing naming and structure standards, and creating the governance framework to sustain the model over time.

---

## The Outcome

- **77% reduction** in documentation volume: from 1.1 million pages to 250,000.
- Eliminated competing search results by consolidating content under single authoritative documents per product.
- Created a consistent, predictable documentation experience customers can rely on across all product lines.
- Established cleaner metadata and improved product-to-documentation linking across the portfolio.
- Built the content foundation that later enabled AI and LLM readiness initiatives — clean, well-structured content is prerequisite to effective AI consumption.

---

## Why It Matters

This project demonstrates what it looks like when information architecture operates at enterprise scale: a research-driven model, implemented across thousands of products, that improves the customer experience while reducing operational overhead. The architecture is still in use today.
